# Requirements

## Must-Have

- Low total cost compared with Fireflies.
- Reliable recording for many meetings per day.
- Ability to record meetings even when transcription is not needed.
- Accurate transcription for real meetings.
- Reliable speaker separation or speaker labels.
- Searchable meeting history.
- Useful summaries and action items.
- Easy export of transcripts and summaries.
- Works with my current meeting workflow.
- Clear privacy and data retention model.

## Nice-To-Have

- Pay only for meetings that need transcription or summarization.
- Separate recording storage from transcription processing.
- Local or self-hosted option.
- No meeting bot required.
- Calendar integration.
- Automatic recording ingestion.
- Good support for multiple accents.
- Markdown export.
- API or automation support.
- Obsidian-friendly output.

## Constraints

- Cost: primary decision criterion; Fireflies is considered expensive.
- Privacy requirements:
- Platforms:
- Meeting tools:
- Storage preferences:
- Automation preferences:
- Meeting volume: many meetings per day.
- Transcription volume: not every meeting needs transcription.

## Questions

- What is the cheapest reliable way to record all or most meetings?
- Can recording and transcription be separated into two tools?
- Do I need live meeting capture, or is post-meeting upload enough?
- Is local transcription worth the extra workflow complexity?
- Do I need summarization built in, or can that be handled by an AI agent afterward?
- How important are integrations compared with owning the raw transcript data?
- What data am I comfortable sending to a cloud vendor?
